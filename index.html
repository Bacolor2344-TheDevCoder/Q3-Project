Today's Blog of the Philippine National Artist for Architecture

Leandro Locsin, the National Artist for Architecture, changed the Philippine urban landscape with a distinct architecture, reflective of Philippine art and culture. It is this synthesis that underlies all his works, with his achievements in concrete reflecting his mastery of space and scale.


Leandro V. Locsin (August 15, 1928-November 15, 1994), also known as LVL in his initials, with his elaborate expressions of the Philippine culture through his building projects, did not only change the local landscape, but also our ways on how to express and promote the Filipino culture domestically and internationally.


In a period from 1955-1994, Locsin has built 155 buildings, among them 75 residential buldings, including the Istana Nurul Iman, the royal residence of Brunei, The CCP Complex, Philippine International Convention Center, and the Sofitel Philippine Plaza.
